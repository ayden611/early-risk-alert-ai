Why t=6.0 matters

t=6.0 is intentionally selective. It is best framed as a conservative telemetry / stepdown review queue optimized for alert-burden reduction, low false positives, and focused command-center review.

Why t=4.0 still matters

t=4.0 is the high-acuity / ICU operating point. It increases detection while still reducing alert burden versus standard threshold-only alerting.

      Clinical Review Language

      ERA prioritizes monitored review context by combining threshold behavior, trend signals, primary drivers, and timing information.

      • Use: “Priority Tier” instead of “diagnosis.”
      • Use: “Primary Driver” instead of “cause.”
      • Use: “May warrant review” instead of “requires intervention.”
      • Use: “Retrospective timing analysis” instead of “proven early detection.”
